About Me

Hello World!

My name is Rocco Vulpis and I recently graduated from Stockton University with a B.S. in Computer Science. Throughout my undergraduate studies, I engaged in a diverse array of projects that not only deepened my understanding of core computer science theories, but also had practical applications. I developed this website to showcase projects I found most interesting or enjoyed working on the most. I am passionate about applying my knowledge to solve real-world problems, particularly in software development.

Courses

During the process of pursuing my degree, there were more than a handful of courses that I think played a crucial role in shaping my understanding and skills in computer science. Below are some of the courses I took that I enrolled in while pursuing my degree.

Data Structures and Algorithms I

Focused on foundational data structures and essential algorithms for data manipulation.

Artificial Intelligence

Introduction to AI principles, including machine learning, reasoning, and neural networks.

Discrete Mathematics

Essential mathematical theories and concepts for computer science, focusing on logical computation.

Data Structures and Algorithms II

Advanced topics in algorithms and data structures, enhancing problem-solving capabilities.

Computer Architecture

Covers the structure and functionality of computer systems and hardware operation.

Probability and Applied Statistics

Introduction to probability theory and statistical methods for analyzing data and making informed decisions.

Software Engineering

Covers software development methodologies, project management, and design patterns for efficient software creation.

Computer Organization

Detailed study of computer components and their interrelationships, including memory systems and processor execution.

Calculus I

Foundational course in differential calculus, focusing on limits, derivatives, and their applications.

Web Applications Engineering

Design and development of modern web applications with a focus on backend and frontend technologies.

Database Systems

Study of database architecture, SQL, and transaction management, focusing on efficient data storage and retrieval.

Calculus II

Continues calculus studies with integral calculus and techniques of integration.

Foundations of Computer Science

Examination of theoretical computer science, automata theory, and computational complexity.

Networking Principles

Exploration of network architectures, protocols, and security measures in digital communication systems.

Calculus III

Advanced calculus topics including multivariable calculus, partial derivatives, and multiple integrals.
